About

This workshop, supported by the Global Centre on Adaptation (GCA) and led by the Alliance of Bioversity International and the International Center for Tropical Agriculture (CIAT) (the Alliance) aims to enhance resilience to climate risks in Africa by linking Early Warning Systems (EWS) with Risk Insurance Mechanisms (RIM). This comes at a critical time when the frequency and severity of climate hazards are escalating, demanding urgent and innovative approaches to risk management. Delivered in collaboration with leading experts from organizations, the training will offer participants the tools and strategies needed to manage climate risks comprehensively. EWS provides critical, timely information to help communities and institutions anticipate climate-related hazards. When paired with RIM, which offers financial protection against losses, these systems form a powerful duo to reduce vulnerability and enhance recovery from climate shocks.

Therefore, the Alliance is planning a 4-day workshop to with the following expected outcomes:

  • Improved ability to design and implement EWS and RIM, with a clear understanding of how these tools complement each other in managing climate risks.
  • Enhanced capacity to apply advanced climate forecasting tools and data-driven decision-making processes in their respective fields, ensuring better preparedness and timely responses to climate-related hazards.
  • Strengthened capacity within their organizations to integrate EWS and RIM into operational frameworks.
  • Established networks with peers and regional experts, promoting knowledge sharing and cross-institutional collaboration to scale up climate resilience efforts.

Target Audience: The training is designed for professionals from national meteorological and disaster management agencies, agricultural ministries and extension services, insurance companies and financial institutions, and development and humanitarian organizations. Participants will be selected to ensure balanced representation and diversity. To facilitate participation, the organizing team will cover economy class return airfare, accommodation, and other workshop-related costs in line with organizational guidelines.
